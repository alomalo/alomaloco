Reserved Channel Waterfront

with Liu Yang
GSD 1211 Landscape Architecture Core III
Critic: Bradley Cantrell
2016

As climate change progresses unchecked, coastal populations can expect to contend with rising sea levels and more devastating storms. While researching the impact of these storms on our site, we noticed that maps that show the impact of these storms focus on human settlements. They are released by government and financial institutions with the goal of making predictions that will help to preserve economic stability and growth, but the exclusion of unsettled plant and animal habitats in these maps suggests a lack of consideration for ecological services that contribute to the stability they seek to preserve. We propose to build a resilient node of the multi-modal global system on this site that is self-sustaining and integrated into the broader regional eco-corridors and industrial flows.

Fan Pier Park

GSD 1111 Landscape Architecture CORE I
Critic: Zaneta Hong
2015

The coastlines of New England are peppered with drumlins left by the glaciers that shaped this area. Many are uninhabited and inaccessible, their caps of deep, dense green rising mysteriously out of the ocean.

The islands of this park and the piers that connect them are meditative spaces that allow appreciation and contemplation of the various sensory phenomena of the sea coast.

East Boston Music High School

MIT SA+P 4.115 Architecture Design Studio II
Critic: William O'Brien Jr.
2014

Conceptual Precedent: At points of connection to the substrate fabric, individual fibers are packed so densely that they fuse, or ‘felt’. The concepts of gathering strands, continuous paths, and significant nodes at turns and intersections persist in the final building scheme.
